Candlestick Graph
Brief History: The candlestick graph was created in the 1700s by Japanese rice farmers to analyze market prices of rice. It was adopted by financial markets, such as Wall Street as a global price chart.
Color of The Candlesticks: The color of each candlestick in the graph represents the movement of the price for the given stock / cryptocurrency with green meaning a positive movement and red meaning a negative movement.
Time Interval: This graph has a time interval of year / month / and day depending on how far you are zoomed in. As you zoom in you will see smaller time intervals and as you zoom out the time intervals will grow.
Open / Close: Each candlestick will show the open and close price of the coin. When the candlestick is green the closing price will be on top and the open price will be on the bottom. Conversely, when the candlestick is red the opening price will be on top and the close price on the bottom. Carefully analyzing the graphs we can also see that the opening price of one day's candlestick will align with the closing price of the prior day.
High / Low: The wick of each candlestick will show the highest and lowest price of a coin during a time interval. The top of the wick represents the highest price the coin was traded for during that time interval and the bottom of the wick represents the lowest price during that time interval.
